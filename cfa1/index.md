## **Registration & Compliance (1-30)**

### **1. If an RIA has between $25 million and $100 million in AUM, where must it register?**
- A) SEC only
- B) State(s) only
- C) Either SEC or state(s), at the adviser's discretion
- D) Both SEC and state(s)

**Answer: C**  
*Under NSMIA, RIAs with $25–$100 million AUM can choose SEC or state registration.*

### **2. What is the primary purpose of Form ADV Part 2A?**
- A) To register with FINRA
- B) To provide clients with a brochure detailing services, fees, and conflicts
- C) To file annual financial statements
- D) To report custody of client assets

**Answer: B**  
*Form ADV Part 2A is the "brochure" delivered to clients.*

### **3. Under the Uniform Securities Act, which is NOT a method of state securities registration?**
- A) Registration by coordination
- B) Registration by notification
- C) Registration by qualification
- D) Registration by exemption

**Answer: D**  
*Registration by exemption is not a registration method; it's an exemption from registration.*

### **4. An IAR moves from one RIA firm to another. Which form must be filed to terminate registration at the old firm?**
- A) Form U4
- B) Form U5
- C) Form ADV-W
- D) Form BR

**Answer: B**  
*Form U5 terminates an individual's registration with a firm.*

### **5. The "brochure rule" requires delivery of Form ADV Part 2A to a prospective client at least how many hours before entering into an advisory contract?**
- A) 24 hours
- B) 48 hours
- C) 72 hours
- D) 96 hours

**Answer: B**  
*48 hours in advance, or at time of contract if client can terminate within 5 business days.*

### **6. Which activity would NOT require registration as an investment adviser?**
- A) Providing personalized investment advice for a fee
- B) Publishing a newsletter with generic market commentary
- C) Managing portfolios for 15 clients with discretion
- D) Advising a pension plan for compensation

**Answer: B**  
*Impersonal advice (like newsletters) generally doesn't require registration.*

### **7. Under state "blue sky laws," which securities are typically exempt from registration?**
- A) Municipal bonds
- B) Corporate bonds issued by out-of-state companies
- C) Mutual funds sold to state residents
- D) Securities issued by banks

**Answer: D**  
*Securities issued by banks are typically exempt under state laws.*

### **8. An RIA has custody of client assets. What additional requirement applies?**
- A) Quarterly surprise audits
- B) Annual surprise audit by an independent CPA
- C) Daily reconciliation by the CCO
- D) Segregation of assets with a broker-dealer

**Answer: B**  
*RIAs with custody must undergo an annual surprise audit.*

### **9. What is the maximum number of non-accredited investors allowed in a Rule 506(b) private placement?**
- A) 10
- B) 35
- C) 100
- D) Unlimited

**Answer: B**  
*Rule 506(b) allows up to 35 non-accredited investors.*

### **10. Which SEC rule prohibits performance-based advertising unless certain conditions are met?**

## **Fiduciary Duty & Ethics (31-70)**

### **31. Under the Advisers Act, an adviser's fiduciary duty includes all EXCEPT:**
- A) Duty of loyalty
- B) Duty of care
- C) Duty to recommend proprietary products
- D) Duty to disclose conflicts

**Answer: C**  
*No duty to recommend proprietary products; may actually be a conflict.*

### **32. Soft-dollar arrangements are permitted under Section 28(e) if:**
- A) The products/services benefit the adviser personally
- B) The products/services provide research or brokerage services
- C) The client explicitly prohibits them
- D) They exceed 5% of total commissions

**Answer: B**  
*Must provide research or brokerage services that benefit clients.*

### **33. When must an adviser obtain client consent for principal trades?**
- A) After the trade is executed
- B) At least annually in writing
- C) At least 10 days before the trade
- D) At the time of account opening

**Answer: B**  
*Written consent must be obtained at least annually.*

### **34. Under the Uniform Prudent Investor Act, a trustee's primary duty is to:**
- A) Maximize income for current beneficiaries
- B) Preserve principal at all costs
- C) Manage the trust as a prudent investor would
- D) Invest only in government securities

**Answer: C**  
*UPIA requires managing as a prudent investor would.*

### **35. Which is NOT a prohibited practice under the Advisers Act?**
- A) Churning
- B) Front-running
- C) Scalping
- D) Dollar-cost averaging

**Answer: D**  
*Dollar-cost averaging is a legitimate strategy.*

### **36. An adviser recommends a security in which she has a material financial interest. What must she disclose?**
- A) Only if the client asks
- B) The nature of the conflict, in writing
- C) Only if the security underperforms
- D) Nothing, if the security is suitable

**Answer: B**  
*Must disclose material conflicts in writing.*

### **37. Under ERISA, a fiduciary is personally liable for:**
- A) Market losses
- B) Breaches of fiduciary duty
- C) Participant investment decisions
- D) Plan administration errors by third parties

**Answer: B**  
*Fiduciaries are personally liable for breaches of duty.*

### **38. The "know your customer" rule (Rule 405) requires brokers to:**
- A) Verify client identity under CIP rules
- B) Collect detailed financial information
- C) Ensure recommendations are suitable
- D) All of the above

**Answer: C**  
*Rule 405 is the "know your customer" suitability rule.*

### **39. Which would violate an adviser's fiduciary duty?**

- B) Disclosing conflicts in Form ADV
- C) Using client commissions to obtain research
- D) Investing in a mutual fund with a 12b-1 fee

### **40. Under the USA, which is considered fraud?**
