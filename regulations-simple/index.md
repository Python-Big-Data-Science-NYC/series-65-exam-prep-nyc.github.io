# Investment Adviser Regulation: 100 MCQs with Explanations

## Section 1: Registration & Definitions

**1. According to NASAA rules, state-registered investment advisers must describe any relationship they have with:**
A) Only SEC-registered broker-dealers
B) Any issuer of securities not already listed in Part 2A
C) All financial institutions they do business with
D) Their top 10 clients

**Answer: B** - State-registered advisers must describe any relationship with any issuer of securities not already listed in Part 2A of their Form ADV.

---

**2. Why are "buy and hold" clients generally unsuitable for wrap fee accounts?**
A) They require more personalized attention
B) They don't trade enough to benefit from bundled commissions
C) Their portfolios are too conservative
D) They typically have smaller account balances

**Answer: B** - Buy-and-hold clients don't engage in sufficient trading activity to justify the wrap fee structure where commissions are included.

---

**3. If a question doesn't specify federal or state registration, which should you assume?**
A) Federal covered adviser
B) State-registered adviser
C) Exempt reporting adviser
D) Municipal adviser

**Answer: B** - For NASAA exams, assume state-registered adviser unless specified otherwise.

---

**4. When must an SEC-registered adviser deliver the firm brochure to a new client?**
A) Within 48 hours after contract signing
B) Within 5 business days of the first trade
C) Before or at the time of entering the advisory agreement
D) Only upon client request

**Answer: C** - SEC rules require delivery before or at the time the advisory agreement is entered into.

---

**5. How often must updated brochures be delivered to clients?**
A) Quarterly
B) Semi-annually
C) Annually within 120 days of fiscal year end
D) Only when material changes occur

**Answer: C** - Annually within 120 days of the end of the fiscal year.

---

## Section 2: Brochure Requirements & Updates

**6. When is NO brochure or summary required at annual update time?**
A) When client assets exceed $1 million
B) When there are no material changes
C) When using a qualified custodian
D) When clients are institutions only

**Answer: B** - If no material changes occurred, nothing needs to be sent.

---

**7. State-registered advisers must deliver brochures at least 48 hours before contract signing unless:**
A) The client is an accredited investor
B) The client has right to terminate within 5 business days without penalty
C) The adviser has over $100 million AUM
D) The client is a family member

**Answer: B** - The 48-hour rule can be waived if the client has a 5-business-day penalty-free termination right.

---

**8. Brochure supplements must be delivered to all clients EXCEPT:**
A) Those receiving only impersonal advice
B) Executive officers of the advisory firm
C) Clients with over $1 million in assets
D) Both A and B

**Answer: D** - Both impersonal advice clients and certain firm executives are exempt from supplement delivery.

---

**9. Which clients are exempt from brochure delivery requirements?**
A) Registered investment companies
B) Clients paying over $5,000 annually
C) Non-profit organizations
D) All institutional clients

**Answer: A** - Registered investment companies are exempt from brochure delivery.

---

**10. Brochures must be updated promptly when:**
A) Client assets increase by 10%
B) Any information becomes materially inaccurate
C) A new employee is hired
D) The market declines significantly

**Answer: B** - Prompt update required when any information becomes materially inaccurate.

---

**11. Federal covered advisers file brochure amendments through:**
A) State securities authorities
B) IARD electronically
C) FINRA Gateway
D) SEC paper filings

**Answer: B** - Electronic filing through IARD is required.

---

**12. State-registered advisers must file brochure supplements with:**
A) SEC only
B) State authorities through IARD
C) FINRA
D) No filing required

**Answer: B** - State authorities through IARD.
