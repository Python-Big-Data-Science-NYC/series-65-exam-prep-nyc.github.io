## **Series 65: Mutual Fund Advanced Practice Questions**

### **Question 1**
A mutual fund's prospectus states it has a 12b-1 fee of 0.25%. This fee is used for:
A. Portfolio management expenses
B. Shareholder servicing and distribution costs
C. Administrative and recordkeeping expenses
D. Custodial and audit fees

**Correct Answer:** B. Shareholder servicing and distribution costs

**Explanation:** 12b-1 fees (named after the SEC rule) are used for distribution expenses, including marketing, advertising, and paying brokers who sell fund shares. They can also include shareholder service fees for maintaining customer accounts.

---

### **Question 2**
Which class of mutual fund shares typically has a front-end sales charge but no 12b-1 fee?
A. Class A shares
B. Class B shares
C. Class C shares
D. Institutional shares

**Correct Answer:** A. Class A shares

**Explanation:** Class A shares usually have front-end sales loads (typically 3-5.75%) but lower annual expenses with little or no 12b-1 fees. They're suitable for long-term investors as the cost is paid upfront.

---

### **Question 3**
A mutual fund's total return over 5 years is 45%. What is its compound annual growth rate (CAGR)?
A. 9.0%
B. 7.7%
C. 8.2%
D. 9.5%

**Correct Answer:** B. 7.7%

**Explanation:** CAGR = [(Ending Value/Beginning Value)^(1/n)] - 1. For 45% total return: (1.45)^(1/5) - 1 = 1.077 - 1 = 7.7%.

---

### **Question 4**
An open-end mutual fund has a net asset value (NAV) of $25.00 per share and 1 million shares outstanding. The fund's total assets are $30 million with $5 million in liabilities. What is the fund's NAV?
A. $25.00
B. $30.00
C. $24.00
D. $26.00

**Correct Answer:** A. $25.00

**Explanation:** NAV = (Total Assets - Liabilities) / Shares Outstanding = ($30M - $5M) / 1M = $25M / 1M = $25.00.

---

### **Question 5**
Which mutual fund pricing method allows investors to buy shares at the next computed NAV?
A. Forward pricing
B. Backward pricing
C. Historical pricing
D. Continuous pricing

**Correct Answer:** A. Forward pricing

**Explanation:** SEC rules require all mutual fund transactions to use forward pricing—investors receive the next computed NAV after their order is received.

---

### **Question 6**
A mutual fund's expense ratio is 1.2%. If the fund earns 8% gross return, what is the investor's net return?
A. 6.8%
B. 7.0%
C. 6.5%
D. 7.2%

**Correct Answer:** A. 6.8%

**Explanation:** Net return = Gross return - Expense ratio = 8% - 1.2% = 6.8%.

---

### **Question 7**
A breakpoint schedule offers reduced sales charges for larger investments. What is the typical breakpoint quantity?
A. $25,000
B. $50,000
C. $100,000
D. $250,000

**Correct Answer:** B. $50,000

**Explanation:** While breakpoints vary by fund, $50,000 is a common initial breakpoint for reduced front-end loads on Class A shares.

---

### **Question 8**
Which statement about money market funds is CORRECT?
A. They typically invest in long-term Treasury bonds
B. They maintain a stable $1.00 NAV per share
C. They offer unlimited FDIC insurance
D. They have no interest rate risk

**Correct Answer:** B. They maintain a stable $1.00 NAV per share

**Explanation:** Money market funds aim to maintain a stable $1.00 NAV (though not guaranteed). They invest in short-term, high-quality debt and are not FDIC insured.

---

### **Question 9**
A mutual fund has a beta of 1.2. If the market rises 10%, the fund would be expected to:
A. Rise 10%
B. Rise 12%
C. Fall 12%
D. Rise 8%

**Correct Answer:** B. Rise 12%

**Explanation:** Beta measures volatility relative to the market. Beta of 1.2 means the fund should move 20% more than the market: 10% × 1.2 = 12%.

---

### **Question 10**
Which document must be delivered to mutual fund investors before or at the time of purchase?
A. Statement of Additional Information (SAI)
B. Annual report
C. Prospectus
D. Semi-annual report

**Correct Answer:** C. Prospectus

**Explanation:** The prospectus must be delivered to investors before or with their purchase confirmation. The SAI must be provided upon request.

---

### **Question 11**
A mutual fund's turnover ratio is 85%. This indicates:
A. 85% of the portfolio was sold during the year
B. The average holding period is 85 months
C. 85% of investors redeemed shares
D. The fund's expenses are 85% of assets

**Correct Answer:** A. 85% of the portfolio was sold during the year

**Explanation:** Turnover ratio = Lesser of purchases or sales / average assets. 85% means substantial trading activity, potentially generating higher capital gains distributions.

---

### **Question 12**
A mutual fund distributes $2.00 per share in capital gains and $1.00 in dividends. An investor in the 15% capital gains bracket and 25% ordinary income bracket would owe:
A. $0.45 per share
B. $0.65 per share
C. $0.30 per share
D. $0.55 per share

**Correct Answer:** B. $0.65 per share

**Explanation:** Capital gains tax: $2.00 × 15% = $0.30. Dividend tax: $1.00 × 25% = $0.25. Total = $0.55. (Note: Qualified dividends might be taxed at capital gains rates, but question specifies ordinary income rate for dividends.)

---

### **Question 13**
Which mutual fund share class typically has a contingent deferred sales charge (CDSC)?
A. Class A
B. Class B
C. Class D
D. Institutional

**Correct Answer:** B. Class B shares

**Explanation:** Class B shares usually have back-end loads (CDSC) that decline over time (e.g., 5% first year, declining to 0% after 6-8 years), and often convert to Class A shares after the CDSC period ends.

---

### **Question 14**
An investor wants to avoid market timing in mutual funds. The SEC requires funds to have policies to prevent:
A. Frequent trading that disadvantages long-term shareholders
B. All trades within 30 days of purchase
C. Any redemption within 90 days
D. Purchases over $100,000

**Correct Answer:** A. Frequent trading that disadvantages long-term shareholders

**Explanation:** SEC rules require funds to have policies to detect and prevent market timing that harms long-term investors through dilution and increased transaction costs.

---

### **Question 15**
A mutual fund's Sharpe ratio measures:
A. Risk-adjusted return
B. Alpha generation
C. Beta relative to market
D. Standard deviation of returns

**Correct Answer:** A. Risk-adjusted return

**Explanation:** Sharpe ratio = (Portfolio return - Risk-free rate) / Standard deviation. Higher ratios indicate better risk-adjusted performance.

---

### **Question 16**
Which investment is NOT suitable for a growth mutual fund?
A. Emerging market stocks
B. Blue-chip dividend payers
C. Technology startups
D. Small-cap growth companies

**Correct Answer:** B. Blue-chip dividend payers

**Explanation:** Growth funds seek capital appreciation, not income. Blue-chip dividend payers are typically value or income investments.

### **Question 17**
A mutual fund has a portfolio of 75% stocks, 20% bonds, and 5% cash. This fund is best classified as:
A. Balanced fund
B. Growth and income fund
C. Asset allocation fund

### **Question 18**
The Investment Company Act of 1940 requires mutual funds to have:
A. At least 100 shareholders
B. A board of directors with 40% independence
C. Daily calculation of NAV
D. Custody of assets with a qualified bank

**Correct Answer:** D. Custody of assets with a qualified bank

### **Question 19**
A mutual fund's alpha of +1.5% indicates:
A. Underperformance by 1.5%
B. Outperformance by 1.5% after adjusting for risk
C. Beta of 1.5
D. Standard deviation of 1.5%

**Correct Answer:** B. Outperformance by 1.5% after adjusting for risk

**Explanation:** Alpha measures excess return relative to a benchmark after adjusting for risk (beta). Positive alpha indicates outperformance.

---

### **Question 20**
Which mutual fund fee is NOT included in the expense ratio?
A. Management fee
B. 12b-1 fee
C. Sales load
D. Administrative expenses

**Correct Answer:** C. Sales load

**Explanation:** The expense ratio includes annual operating expenses (management fees, 12b-1 fees, administrative costs) but excludes sales loads (front-end, back-end, or level loads).

---

A. Large-cap fund

### **Question 23**
A mutual fund's standard deviation is 15%. This measures:

### **Question 25**
A mutual fund's R-squared of 95 indicates:
A. 95% of returns are explained by market movements
B. The fund outperformed by 95 basis points
C. The expense ratio is 0.95%
D. 95% of assets are in cash

**Correct Answer:** A. 95% of returns are explained by market movements

**Explanation:** R-squared measures how much of a fund's performance is explained by its benchmark (0-100%). 95% means the fund closely tracks its benchmark.

### **Question 27**
A mutual fund's NAV increases from $20 to $22 and distributes $1 in capital gains. What is the total return?
A. 10%
B. 15%
C. 12%
D. 13%

**Correct Answer:** B. 15%

**Explanation:** Total return = (Ending NAV - Beginning NAV + Distributions) / Beginning NAV = ($22 - $20 + $1) / $20 = $3/$20 = 15%.

### **Question 28**
The "4pm cutoff" for mutual fund transactions refers to:
A. Time orders must be received to get that day's NAV
B. Time prospectuses must be delivered
C. Deadline for breakpoint eligibility
D. Time for calculating expense ratios

**Correct Answer:** A. Time orders must be received to get that day's NAV

**Explanation:** Mutual funds typically price once daily at 4pm Eastern Time. Orders received by this time receive that day's NAV; later orders get the next day's NAV.

### **Question 38**
Which mutual fund structure avoids double taxation?
A. Corporation
B. Trust
C. Partnership

### **Question 40**
The maximum sales charge on a mutual fund with a 5% load and $10 NAV is:
A. $0.50 per share
B. $0.53 per share
C. $0.48 per share
D. $0.55 per share

**Correct Answer:** B. $0.53 per share

**Explanation:** Load is calculated as percentage of offering price. For 5% load: Offering price = NAV / (1 - load%) = $10 / 0.95 = $10.53. Load = $0.53.
