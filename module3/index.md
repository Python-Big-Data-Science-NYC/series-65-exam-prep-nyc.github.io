## Part 1: Equity Securities (Questions 1-50)

### **Question 1**
**Question:** Common stockholders have:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Voting rights and residual claim to assets
B. Fixed dividend payments
C. Priority over creditors in liquidation
D. Guaranteed capital appreciation
**Correct Answer:** A. Voting rights and residual claim to assets
**Explanation:** Common stockholders typically have voting rights and receive remaining assets after all debts and preferred claims are settled in liquidation.

---

### **Question 2**
**Question:** Preferred stock differs from common stock in that preferred stock:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Has voting rights
B. Pays variable dividends
C. Has priority in dividend payments and liquidation
D. Participates in company growth
**Correct Answer:** C. Has priority in dividend payments and liquidation
**Explanation:** Preferred stockholders receive dividends before common stockholders and have higher priority in liquidation, but usually lack voting rights.

---

### **Question 3**
**Question:** Cumulative preferred stock means:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Dividends accumulate if not paid
B. Dividends increase each year
C. Stock converts to common after time
D. Voting rights accumulate
**Correct Answer:** A. Dividends accumulate if not paid
**Explanation:** Unpaid dividends on cumulative preferred stock accumulate and must be paid before common dividends.

---

### **Question 4**
**Question:** Participating preferred stock allows holders to:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Vote on all matters
B. Receive additional dividends beyond stated rate
C. Convert to common at any time
D. Receive dividends quarterly
**Correct Answer:** B. Receive additional dividends beyond stated rate
**Explanation:** Participating preferred shareholders receive their fixed dividend plus additional dividends if common dividends exceed certain levels.

---

### **Question 5**
**Question:** A company's book value per share is calculated as:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Market price × shares outstanding
B. Total assets ÷ shares outstanding
C. (Total assets - Total liabilities) ÷ shares outstanding
D. Net income ÷ shares outstanding
**Correct Answer:** C. (Total assets - Total liabilities) ÷ shares outstanding
**Explanation:** Book value = (Assets - Liabilities) ÷ Shares outstanding = Shareholders' equity ÷ Shares outstanding.

## Part 2: Fixed Income Securities (Questions 51-100)

### **Question 51**
**Question:** The coupon rate on a bond is:
**Answer Choices:**
A. The market interest rate
B. The stated annual interest rate
C. The yield to maturity
D. The current yield
**Correct Answer:** B. The stated annual interest rate
**Explanation:** Coupon rate is fixed percentage of face value paid annually; contractually stated at issuance.

---

### **Question 52**
**Question:** When a bond is issued at a discount:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Coupon rate < Market rate
B. Coupon rate > Market rate
C. Price > Face value
D. Yield < Coupon rate
**Correct Answer:** A. Coupon rate < Market rate
**Explanation:** Bonds sell at discount when coupon rate is below prevailing market rates.

### **Question 53**

**Answer Choices:**
A. Annual interest ÷ Market price
B. Annual interest ÷ Par value
C. Total return ÷ Years to maturity
D. YTM - Coupon rate
**Correct Answer:** A. Annual interest ÷ Market price
**Explanation:** Current yield = Annual interest payment ÷ Current market price.

### **Question 54**
**Question:** Yield to maturity (YTM) considers:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Only current income
B. Interest payments and capital gain/loss at maturity
C. Only coupon payments
D. Inflation adjustments
**Correct Answer:** B. Interest payments and capital gain/loss at maturity
**Explanation:** YTM is total return if held to maturity, including all coupon payments and amortization of premium/discount.

### **Question 55**

**Answer Choices:**
A. Pay interest semiannually
B. Are issued at par
C. Are sold at deep discount
D. Have variable rates
**Correct Answer:** C. Are sold at deep discount
**Explanation:** Zeros pay no periodic interest; return comes from difference between purchase price and face value at maturity.

### **Question 56**
**Question:** Duration measures:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Time to maturity
B. Interest rate risk sensitivity
C. Credit quality
D. Yield curve position
**Correct Answer:** B. Interest rate risk sensitivity
**Explanation:** Duration estimates price change for 1% interest rate change; higher duration = greater interest rate risk.

### **Question 71-90: Quick Fixed Income Questions**

**71. "Bond ladder" strategy:** Purchases bonds with staggered maturities to manage reinvestment risk

**72. "Barbell strategy":** Concentrates in short-term and long-term bonds, avoiding intermediates

**73. "Bullet strategy":** Concentrates in bonds with similar maturities

**74. "Spread" over Treasuries measures:** Additional yield for credit/liquidity risk

**75. "Yield curve" shows:** Relationship between yields and maturities

**76. Normal yield curve:** Upward sloping (longer maturities = higher yields)

**77. Inverted yield curve:** Downward sloping (often predicts recession)

**78. Flat yield curve:** Little difference between short and long rates

**79. "Credit spread risk":** Risk that spread between yields widens

**80. "Reinvestment risk":** Risk that coupons/called principal must be reinvested at lower rates

**81. "Interest rate risk":** Bond prices move inversely to interest rates

**82. "Default risk":** Risk issuer fails to make payments

**83. "Liquidity risk":** Risk bond cannot be sold quickly at fair price

**84. "Inflation risk":** Purchasing power erosion

**85. "Event risk":** Specific events affecting issuer credit

**86. "Sinking fund provision":** Requires issuer to retire portion of bonds periodically

**87. "Debentures" are:** Unsecured bonds backed only by issuer's general credit

**88. "Subordinated debentures" have:** Lower priority than other debt in liquidation

**89. "Collateral trust bonds" secured by:** Financial assets (stocks, bonds) pledged as collateral

**90. "Equipment trust certificates" finance:** Equipment (airplanes, railcars) with equipment as collateral

### **Question 91-100: Bond Calculations & Concepts**

**91. Price of bond selling at 95:** 95% of face value = $950 per $1,000 bond

**92. Basis point value:** 0.01% (100 basis points = 1%)

**93. Bond equivalent yield formula:** [(Face - Price) ÷ Price] × (365 ÷ Days to maturity)

**94. Taxable equivalent yield formula:** Municipal yield ÷ (1 - Tax rate)

**95. Accrued interest calculation:** (Annual coupon ÷ Payment frequency) × (Days since last payment ÷ Days in period)

**96. "Clean price" of bond:** Price without accrued interest

**97. "Dirty price" of bond:** Price including accrued interest (actual transaction price)

**98. "Street convention" for day count:** Typically 30/360 for corporates, actual/actual for governments

**99. "Z-spread" measures:** Spread over Treasury zero-coupon yield curve

**100. "Option-adjusted spread" (OAS):** Spread adjusted for embedded options (calls, puts)

C. Stock price - Strike price (if positive)

**Correct Answer:** C. Stock price - Strike price (if positive)
**Explanation:** Intrinsic value = difference between stock price and strike price when in-the-money; zero if out-of-money.

### **Question 166-185: Quick Alternative Investment Questions**

**166. "Long straddle" strategy:** Buy call and put with same strike/expiration (profits from large move either direction)

**167. "Short straddle" strategy:** Sell call and put with same strike/expiration (profits from low volatility)

**168. "Bull spread" using calls:** Buy lower strike call, sell higher strike call (limits cost and profit)

**169. "Bear spread" using puts:** Buy higher strike put, sell lower strike put

**170. "Butterfly spread" combines:** Bull and bear spreads for limited risk/return

**171. "Iron condor" combines:** Bull put spread and bear call spread

**172. "Delta" measures:** Option price change for $1 stock change

**173. "Gamma" measures:** Rate of delta change

**174. "Theta" measures:** Time decay (daily option value loss)

**175. "Vega" measures:** Sensitivity to volatility changes

**176. "Rho" measures:** Sensitivity to interest rate changes

**177. "In the money" call:** Stock price > Strike price

**178. "At the money":** Stock price = Strike price

**179. "Out of the money" call:** Stock price < Strike price

**180. "American style" options:** Can be exercised any time before expiration

**181. "European style" options:** Can be exercised only at expiration

**182. "Futures contango":** Future price > Spot price (normal for non-perishables)

**183. "Futures backwardation":** Future price < Spot price (common for perishables)

**184. "Rolling futures" means:** Closing expiring contract and opening later expiration

**185. "Cash-settled" derivatives settle in:** Cash, not physical delivery

### **Question 186-200: Derivatives & Strategies**

**186. "Swaps" involve:** Exchanging cash flows (interest rates, currencies, etc.)

**187. "Interest rate swap" typically:** Fixed rate payments exchanged for floating rate

**188. "Credit default swap" (CDS) provides:** Insurance against bond/default risk

**189. "Total return swap" exchanges:** Total return of asset for floating rate + spread

**190. "Forward contracts" are:** Customized OTC agreements (not exchange-traded)

**191. "Arbitrage" seeks:** Risk-free profits from price discrepancies

**192. "Hedging" reduces:** Specific risks by taking offsetting positions

**193. "Speculation" involves:** Taking risk for potential profit

**194. "Leverage" in derivatives:** Small capital controls large position (amplifies gains/losses)

**195. "Counterparty risk" is:** Risk other party defaults on obligation

**196. "Systemic risk" affects:** Entire financial system

**197. "Volatility smile" shows:** Implied volatility varies with strike price

**198. "Black-Scholes model" prices:** European options using stock price, strike, time, rates, volatility

**199. "Binomial model" prices:** Options by modeling possible price paths

**200. "Monte Carlo simulation" models:** Numerous random price paths for valuation

## Summary & Key Features

### **Equity Securities:**
- Common stock: Voting, residual claim, variable dividends
- Preferred stock: Fixed dividends, priority in liquidation, usually non-voting
- ETFs: Exchange-traded, intraday pricing, tax-efficient
- REITs: ≥90% income distribution, real estate exposure

### **Fixed Income:**
- Bond prices inversely related to interest rates
- Duration measures interest rate sensitivity
- Credit ratings assess default risk (AAA to D)
- Munis: Tax-exempt interest, GO vs. revenue bonds

### **Pooled Investments:**
- Mutual funds: Open-end (NAV pricing), Closed-end (exchange trading)
- Hedge funds: Accredited investors, leverage, complex strategies
- Expense ratios, loads, 12b-1 fees
- Index funds vs. active management

### **Alternative Investments:**
- Options: Calls (right to buy), Puts (right to sell)
- Futures: Standardized, exchange-traded, marked-to-market
- Derivatives: Leverage amplifies risk/reward
- Real estate, commodities, collectibles: Low liquidity, specialized knowledge

### **Insurance Products:**
- Fixed annuities: Guaranteed returns
- Variable annuities: Investment-linked, tax-deferred
- Life insurance: Term (temporary), Whole/Universal (permanent with cash value)
- Suitability critical for insurance-based investments
