## Part 1: Uniform Securities Act (USA) - State Regulations (Questions 1-50)

### **Question 1**
**Question:** The Uniform Securities Act (USA) is a model law that:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Has been adopted identically by all 50 states
B. Serves as a template for state securities regulation (Blue Sky Laws)
C. Preempts all federal securities laws
D. Only applies to investment advisers with over $100M AUM
**Correct Answer:** B. Serves as a template for state securities regulation (Blue Sky Laws)
**Explanation:** The USA is a model law developed to create consistency among state securities regulations (Blue Sky Laws), but states may modify it.

---

### **Question 2**
**Question:** Under the USA, which of the following is NOT a defined term that requires registration?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Broker-dealer
B. Investment adviser
C. Investment adviser representative
D. Financial planner
**Correct Answer:** D. Financial planner
**Explanation:** "Financial planner" is not a defined term requiring registration under the USA. Registration requirements apply to broker-dealers, investment advisers, and their representatives.

---

### **Question 3**
**Question:** The "de minimis" exemption under the USA allows an investment adviser to avoid state registration if:
**Answer Choices:**
A. They have no place of business in the state AND have fewer than 6 clients in the state in 12 months
B. They only manage portfolios over $1 million
C. They are SEC-registered regardless of client count
D. They only advise institutional clients
**Correct Answer:** A. They have no place of business in the state AND have fewer than 6 clients in the state in 12 months
**Explanation:** This "de minimis" or "private adviser" exemption allows IAs without a physical presence in a state to avoid registration if they have fewer than 6 clients (not counting certain institutional clients) in that state.

---

### **Question 4**
**Question:** Under the USA, which securities are typically EXEMPT from registration?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Securities issued by the U.S. government
B. Common stock of publicly traded companies
C. Mutual funds
D. Variable annuities
**Correct Answer:** A. Securities issued by the U.S. government
**Explanation:** Government securities (federal, state, municipal) are generally exempt from state registration requirements.

---

### **Question 5**
**Question:** Which transaction is EXEMPT from registration under the USA?
**Answer Choices:**
A. An isolated non-issuer transaction by a broker-dealer
B. A public offering of securities by a startup company
C. An initial public offering (IPO)
D. A secondary market transaction by an investment adviser
**Correct Answer:** A. An isolated non-issuer transaction by a broker-dealer
**Explanation:** Isolated non-issuer transactions (secondary market trades not involving the issuing company) are typically exempt.

---

### **Question 6**
**Question:** The Administrator under the USA refers to:
**Answer Choices:**
A. The SEC Commissioner
B. The state official responsible for securities regulation
C. The head of FINRA
D. The President of the United States
**Correct Answer:** B. The state official responsible for securities regulation
**Explanation:** Each state designates an "Administrator" (often the Secretary of State or securities commissioner) to enforce state securities laws.

---

### **Question 7**
**Question:** Under the USA, investment adviser records must be maintained for:
**Answer Choices:**
A. 1 year
B. 3 years
C. 5 years
D. 7 years
**Correct Answer:** C. 5 years
**Explanation:** The USA generally requires investment advisers to maintain books and records for at least 5 years (first 2 years in an easily accessible place).

---

### **Question 8**
**Question:** Which of the following is a prohibited practice under the USA's anti-fraud provisions?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Recommending a security based on thorough research
B. Omitting material facts when recommending a security
C. Charging a performance fee to a qualified client
D. Using model portfolios for multiple clients
**Correct Answer:** B. Omitting material facts when recommending a security
**Explanation:** Omitting material facts constitutes fraud under the USA, just as much as making false statements.

### **Question 9**

### **Question 10**

**Answer Choices:**

### **Question 21-40: Quick USA Questions**

**21. "Blue Sky Laws" nickname comes from:** Early 20th century concern about speculative schemes with no more basis than "blue sky"

**22. USA developed by:** National Conference of Commissioners on Uniform State Laws (NCCUSL)

**23. Most recent USA revision:** 2002 (though states adopt various versions)

**24. "Notice filing" for federal covered advisers:** Required in states where they have clients, even if exempt from full state registration

**25. "Institutional investor" definition typically includes:** Banks, insurance companies, registered investment companies, employee benefit plans with assets > $1M

**26. "Accredited investor" under USA generally follows:** SEC definition (income > $200k/$300k joint, net worth > $1M excluding primary residence)

**27. "Non-issuer transaction" means:** Secondary market trade not involving the issuing company

**28. "Fraud" under USA includes:** Any device, scheme, or artifice to defraud, or any untrue statement of material fact

**29. Administrator's investigatory powers include:** Subpoena power, ability to compel testimony, access to records

**30. Criminal penalties under USA can include:** Fines and imprisonment (varies by state)

**31. "Post-registration" requirements include:** Maintaining records, filing updates, paying fees

**32. "Successor registration" allows:** Continuation of registration after business changes (merger, consolidation)

**33. "Withdrawal from registration" requires:** Filing form, may be granted unless proceedings pending

**34. "Statutory disqualification" may result from:** Certain criminal convictions, court injunctions, SEC/FINRA disciplinary actions

**35. "Client's rights of rescission" under USA:** Client may void contract if adviser violates certain provisions

**36. "Performance-based fees" allowed for:** Qualified clients under SEC rules (generally $1.1M+ with adviser or $2.2M+ net worth)

**37. "Advertising" rules prohibit:** False or misleading statements, testimonials, past specific recommendations without disclosure

**38. "Solicitors" must disclose:** Their compensation arrangement and relationship with adviser

**39. "Custody rule" exceptions include:** Custody solely due to deduction of fees, certain related person custody arrangements

**40. "Books and records" required include:** Financial statements, client agreements, advisory correspondence, trade records

### **Question 41-50: USA Registration & Exemptions**

**41. Federal covered advisers register with:** SEC, but file notice with states where they have clients

**42. "Venture capital fund advisers" exemption:** Advisers solely to venture capital funds may be exempt from registration

**43. "Private fund adviser" exemption:** Advisers solely to private funds with less than $150M AUM may be exempt

**44. "Intrastate offering" exemption:** Securities offered and sold only to residents of one state

**45. "Regulation D" offerings:** Federal private placement rules often coordinated with state exemptions

**46. "Rule 506" of Reg D:** Unlimited amount to accredited investors + up to 35 sophisticated non-accredited

**47. "Rule 504" of Reg D:** Up to $10M in 12 months, general solicitation allowed in some cases

**48. "Rule 147" intrastate offering:** Safe harbor for intrastate offerings under Securities Act

**49. "Crowdfunding exemption" (Regulation CF):** Up to $5M in 12 months through registered portals

**50. "Regulation A" offerings:** Two tiers: Tier 1 up to $20M, Tier 2 up to $75M with ongoing reporting

## Part 2: Federal Securities Laws (Questions 51-100)

### **Question 51**
**Question:** The Securities Act of 1933 primarily regulates:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Secondary market trading
B. Initial offerings of securities to the public
C. Broker-dealer operations
D. Investment adviser conduct
**Correct Answer:** B. Initial offerings of securities to the public
**Explanation:** The 1933 Act governs the initial distribution (primary market) of securities, requiring registration and disclosure.

---

### **Question 52**
**Question:** The registration statement filed under the 1933 Act becomes effective:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Immediately upon filing
B. After 20 calendar days automatically
C. When the SEC declares it effective
D. After state approval
**Correct Answer:** C. When the SEC declares it effective
**Explanation:** The registration statement becomes effective only when the SEC declares it so, after reviewing for completeness.

---

### **Question 53**
**Question:** During the "waiting period" between filing and effectiveness under the 1933 Act:
**Answer Choices:**
A. No offers or sales may be made
B. Oral offers may be made, but written offers only via preliminary prospectus
C. Sales may be made with a final prospectus
D. Only institutional sales are permitted
**Correct Answer:** B. Oral offers may be made, but written offers only via preliminary prospectus
**Explanation:** In the waiting period, oral offers are permitted, but written offers must be made via preliminary ("red herring") prospectus.

---

### **Question 54**
**Question:** The "prospectus" required by the 1933 Act must contain:
**Answer Choices:**
A. All material information about the offering
B. Only the company's financial statements
C. Marketing materials only
D. No specific requirements
**Correct Answer:** A. All material information about the offering
**Explanation:** The prospectus must contain all material facts necessary for investors to make informed decisions.

---

### **Question 55**
**Question:** Which is NOT an exemption from 1933 Act registration?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Private placements to accredited investors
B. Intrastate offerings
C. Offerings under $5 million
D. Government securities
**Correct Answer:** C. Offerings under $5 million
**Explanation:** Size alone doesn't exempt; Regulation A allows smaller public offerings with reduced requirements, but they're not fully exempt.

### **Question 56**
**Question:** The Securities Exchange Act of 1934 primarily regulates:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Initial public offerings
B. Secondary market trading and market participants

**Correct Answer:** B. Secondary market trading and market participants
**Explanation:** The 1934 Act governs ongoing reporting, secondary market trading, exchanges, brokers, and self-regulatory organizations.

### **Question 57**

**Answer Choices:**
A. Register with the SEC and file periodic reports

**Correct Answer:** B. Any manipulative or deceptive device in connection with securities transactions

### **Question 59**

**Answer Choices:**

## Summary & Key Provisions

### **Uniform Securities Act Key Points:**
1. Model state securities law (Blue Sky Laws)
2. Registers broker-dealers, IAs, IARs, securities, offerings
3. Anti-fraud provisions apply to all securities transactions
4. Administrator has broad enforcement powers
5. Exemptions for certain securities/transactions

### **Federal Securities Laws:**
1. 1933 Act: Primary market, registration, prospectus
2. 1934 Act: Secondary market, reporting, anti-fraud (Rule 10b-5)
3. Advisers Act: IA regulation, fiduciary duty, compliance
4. Dodd-Frank: Post-crisis reforms, Volcker Rule, CFPB
5. Sarbanes-Oxley: Corporate governance, PCAOB

### **Investment Advisers Act Core Rules:**
1. Fiduciary duty (loyalty & care)
2. Anti-fraud provisions (Section 206)
3. Registration & reporting (Form ADV)
4. Compliance program (Rule 206(4)-7)
5. Custody rules (surprise audits)
6. Advertising restrictions
7. Performance fee limitations

### **ERISA Basics:**
1. Governs private employer benefit plans
2. Imposes fiduciary duties (prudent expert, exclusive benefit)
3. Prohibits transactions with parties in interest
4. Requires disclosures (SPD, Form 5500)
5. PBGC insures defined benefit plans

### **Ethical Practices:**
1. Fiduciary duty paramount
2. Full disclosure of conflicts
3. Confidentiality protections
4. Fair allocation practices
5. Best execution obligation
6. Personal trading restrictions
7. Compliance with all applicable laws
