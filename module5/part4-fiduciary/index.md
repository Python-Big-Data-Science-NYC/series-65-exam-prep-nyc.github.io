# ⚖️ Part 4: Fiduciary Duty & Ethics

## **The Highest Standard: Fiduciary Duty**

### Definition:
Investment advisers have a **fiduciary duty** to their clients.

This means:
> The client's interests come first.
> Always.
> No exceptions.

## **Two Components of Fiduciary Duty**

### 1. Duty of Loyalty
- Put client interests ahead of your own
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Disclose all material conflicts
- Seek best execution for clients
- Charge reasonable fees

### 2. Duty of Care
- Act with prudence and diligence
- Make suitable recommendations
- Conduct reasonable investigation
- Monitor investments appropriately
- Use professional competence

## **Investment Advisers Act of 1940**

### Who It Regulates:
- **Investment advisers** (firms)
- **Investment adviser representatives** (individuals)
- Anyone providing investment advice for compensation

### Key Requirements:

#### 1. Registration
- Must register with SEC or state
- Based on assets under management
- Exemptions for certain advisers

#### 2. Fiduciary Duty
- Imposed by law
- Cannot be waived by client
- Applies to all advisory relationships

#### 3. Disclosure
- Form ADV (Parts 1, 2, 3)
- Brochure delivery to clients
- Material changes disclosure

#### 4. Advertising Rules
- No false or misleading statements
- No testimonials (with exceptions)
- No past specific recommendations without criteria
- No guaranteed results

#### 5. Custody Rules
- Special requirements if holding client assets
- Quarterly account statements
- Annual surprise audits
- Notice to clients

#### 6. Recordkeeping
- Maintain books and records
- Retention periods (5+ years)
- Available for inspection

## **Suitability vs. Best Interest**

### Suitability Standard (Broker-Dealers):
- Recommendation must be suitable for client
- Based on client's financial situation and needs
- At the time of recommendation

### Best Interest Standard (Investment Advisers - Fiduciary):
- Must act in client's best interest
- Not just suitable - must be optimal
- Ongoing duty throughout relationship
- Higher standard than suitability

### Critical Difference:
- **Suitable** = Not harmful to client
- **Best interest** = Positively beneficial to client

## **Conflict of Interest Management**

### Types of Conflicts:

#### 1. Compensation Conflicts
- Higher commissions for certain products
- Revenue sharing arrangements
- Soft dollar benefits
- Proprietary product incentives

#### 2. Personal Conflicts
- Trading ahead of clients (front-running)
- Personal investments conflicting with advice
- Family member benefits
- Gifts and entertainment

#### 3. Business Conflicts
- Recommending affiliate products
- Cross-selling services
- Referral fees
- Favoring certain custodians

### Required Actions:
1. **Identify** all conflicts
2. **Disclose** all material conflicts
3. **Manage** conflicts to prevent harm
4. **Mitigate** unavoidable conflicts
5. **Document** conflict handling

## **Code of Ethics Requirements**

### Rule 204A-1 Requirements:
Advisers must adopt and enforce a written code of ethics that includes:
- Standards of business conduct
- Compliance with federal securities laws
- Personal trading rules for access persons
- Reporting of violations
- Annual acknowledgment of code

### Personal Trading Rules:
- Pre-approval for certain trades
- Blackout periods around client trades
- Restricted securities lists
- Holding periods
- Reporting requirements

## **Custody of Client Assets**

### Definition of Custody:
- Holding client funds or securities
- Having authority to withdraw client funds
- Acting as trustee or general partner
- Having power of attorney

### Special Requirements if You Have Custody:
1. **Qualified custodian** must hold assets (bank, broker-dealer)
2. **Quarterly statements** sent to clients
3. **Surprise annual audit** by independent accountant
4. **Internal controls** to protect assets
5. **Notice filing** with regulators

## **Advertising and Marketing Rules**

### Prohibited Practices:
- **Testimonials** (with limited exceptions)
- **Past specific recommendations** without full list and criteria
- **References to past performance** without required disclosures
- **Claims of approval or endorsement** by regulators
- **Guarantees** of any kind

### Required Disclosures in Advertising:
- "Past performance does not guarantee future results"
- Investment adviser registration status
- How performance was calculated
- Fees and expenses
- Material assumptions or limitations

## **Form ADV Requirements**

### Part 1: Filing Information
- Business information
- Ownership structure
- Affiliations
- Disciplinary history

### Part 2: Brochure
- Services offered and fees
- Investment strategies
- Risks involved
- Conflicts of interest
- Disciplinary information
- Educational and business background

### Part 3: Relationship Summary (Form CRS)
- Standardized summary for retail investors
- Plain English format
- Key questions and answers
- Comparison of services

## **Memory Rules for the Exam**

### Rule 1:
> "Client first, last, and always."

### Rule 2:
> "Suitable is not enough - must be best."

### Rule 3:
> "Disclose conflicts or become conflicted."

### Rule 4:
> "Custody means responsibility - handle with care."

### Rule 5:
> "Paper trail protects everyone - document everything."

## **Ethical Decision Framework**

### Questions to Ask:
1. Is this in my client's best interest?
2. Would my client approve if fully informed?
3. Am I treating all clients fairly?
4. Have I disclosed all material information?
5. Would this decision withstand regulatory scrutiny?
6. Does this align with my professional obligations?

### The Golden Test:
> "Would I be comfortable explaining this decision to a regulator, in writing, with my name attached?"
