## Part 1: Basic Economic Concepts (Questions 1-60)

### **Question 1**
**Question:** The law of demand states that:
**Answer Choices:**
A. As price increases, quantity demanded increases
B. As price increases, quantity demanded decreases
C. Price has no effect on quantity demanded
D. Supply determines demand
**Correct Answer:** B. As price increases, quantity demanded decreases
**Explanation:** Inverse relationship between price and quantity demanded (all else equal).

---

### **Question 2**
**Question:** The law of supply states that:
**Answer Choices:**
A. As price increases, quantity supplied decreases
B. As price increases, quantity supplied increases
C. Price has no effect on supply
D. Demand determines supply
**Correct Answer:** B. As price increases, quantity supplied increases
**Explanation:** Direct relationship between price and quantity supplied (all else equal).

**Answer Choices:**

### **Question 41-60: Quick Economic Concepts**

**41. "Supply shock" example:** Sudden oil price increase

**42. "Demand shock" example:** Sudden consumer confidence collapse

**43. "Crowding out" occurs when:** Government borrowing raises rates, reducing private investment

**44. "Liquidity trap" situation:** Rates near zero, monetary policy ineffective

**45. "Velocity of money" measures:** How quickly money circulates

**46. "Money supply M1 includes:** Currency + checkable deposits

**47. "Money supply M2 includes:** M1 + savings deposits, small time deposits

**48. "Phillips curve" shows:** Inverse relationship between inflation and unemployment (short-run)

**49. "Laffer curve" shows:** Relationship between tax rates and tax revenue

**50. "Comparative advantage" means:** Producing at lower opportunity cost

**51. "Absolute advantage" means:** More efficient production overall

**52. "Trade deficit" occurs when:** Imports > Exports

**53. "Trade surplus" occurs when:** Exports > Imports

**54. "Protectionism" involves:** Tariffs, quotas to protect domestic industries

**55. "Free trade" benefits:** Consumer choice, efficiency, lower prices

**56. "Balance of payments" records:** All international transactions

**57. "Current account" includes:** Trade balance, investment income, transfers

**58. "Capital account" includes:** Financial asset transactions

**59. "Foreign exchange rates" determine:** Currency conversion values

**60. "Appreciating currency" makes:** Imports cheaper, exports more expensive

### **Question 91-120: Quick Financial Ratios & Analysis**

**92. Operating margin =** Operating income ÷ Revenue

**93. Net profit margin =** Net income ÷ Revenue

**94. Return on assets (ROA) =** Net income ÷ Total assets

**95. Return on equity (ROE) =** Net income ÷ Shareholders' equity

**96. Asset turnover =** Revenue ÷ Total assets

**97. Inventory turnover =** COGS ÷ Average inventory

**98. Days sales in inventory =** 365 ÷ Inventory turnover

**99. Receivables turnover =** Revenue ÷ Average receivables

**100. Days sales outstanding =** 365 ÷ Receivables turnover

**101. Payables turnover =** COGS ÷ Average payables

**102. Days payable outstanding =** 365 ÷ Payables turnover

**103. Cash conversion cycle =** DSI + DSO - DPO

**104. Times interest earned =** EBIT ÷ Interest expense

**105. Fixed charge coverage =** (EBIT + Lease payments) ÷ (Interest + Lease payments)

**106. Dividend payout ratio =** Dividends per share ÷ EPS

**107. Retention ratio =** 1 - Payout ratio

**108. Price-to-earnings (P/E) =** Price per share ÷ EPS

**109. Price-to-book (P/B) =** Price per share ÷ Book value per share

**110. Price-to-sales (P/S) =** Market cap ÷ Revenue

**111. Price-to-cash flow =** Price per share ÷ Cash flow per share

**112. Enterprise value (EV) =** Market cap + Debt - Cash

**113. EV/EBITDA ratio compares:** Total company value to operating earnings

**114. DuPont analysis breaks ROE into:** Profit margin × Asset turnover × Equity multiplier

**115. Horizontal analysis compares:** Financial statement items over time

**116. Vertical analysis expresses:** Each item as percentage of base (e.g., all income statement items as % of revenue)

**117. Common-size statements facilitate:** Comparison across companies/sizes

### **Question 151-180: Quick Quantitative Methods**

**151. Geometric mean return =** [(1+R1)×(1+R2)×...×(1+Rn)]^(1/n) - 1

**152. Arithmetic mean return =** (R1 + R2 + ... + Rn) ÷ n

**153. Dollar-weighted return =** IRR of cash flows

**154. Time-weighted return =** Geometric mean of period returns (eliminates cash flow effects)

**155. Holding period return =** (Ending value - Beginning value + Income) ÷ Beginning value

**156. Annualized return =** (1 + Total return)^(1/years) - 1

**157. Real return =** [(1 + Nominal) ÷ (1 + Inflation)] - 1

**158. After-tax return =** Pre-tax return × (1 - Tax rate)

**159. Compounded annual growth rate (CAGR) =** (Ending value ÷ Beginning value)^(1/n) - 1

**160. Present value of growing perpetuity =** Payment ÷ (r - g) where g < r

**161. Loan amortization formula calculates:** Equal payments covering interest and principal

**162. Weighted average cost of capital (WACC) =** (E/V)×Re + (D/V)×Rd×(1-Tc)

**163. Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM):** Re = Rf + β(Rm - Rf)

**164. Discounted cash flow (DCF) valuation =** Σ [CFt ÷ (1+r)^t]

**165. Gordon growth model =** D1 ÷ (r - g)

**166. Margin of safety in value investing =** (Intrinsic value - Market price) ÷ Intrinsic value

**167. Z-score for normal distribution:** (x - μ) ÷ σ

**168. Confidence interval range:** Estimate ± (Z-value × Standard error)

**169. Hypothesis testing p-value:** Probability of observing result if null hypothesis true

**170. Regression analysis estimates:** Relationship between dependent and independent variables

**171. Multicollinearity occurs when:** Independent variables highly correlated

**172. Autocorrelation occurs when:** Error terms correlated over time

**173. Heteroskedasticity occurs when:** Variance of errors not constant

**174. Time series analysis examines:** Data points over time intervals

**175. Moving average smooths:** Short-term fluctuations

**176. Exponential smoothing gives more weight to:** Recent observations

**177. Seasonality refers to:** Patterns repeating at regular intervals

**178. Cyclical patterns relate to:** Economic cycles (longer than seasonal)

**179. Random walk hypothesis suggests:** Price changes unpredictable, follow random pattern

**180. Efficient market hypothesis (EMH) states:** Prices reflect all available information

### **Question 211-250: Quick Risk Management Concepts**

**211. Risk tolerance vs risk capacity:** Tolerance = psychological willingness; Capacity = financial ability to bear loss

**212. Risk premium:** Extra return demanded for taking risk

**213. Equity risk premium:** Expected stock return over risk-free rate

**214. Market risk premium:** Expected market return minus risk-free rate

**215. Diversification reduces:** Unsystematic risk (not systematic)

**216. Efficient frontier:** Optimal portfolios for given risk level

**217. Capital market line (CML):** Risk-return tradeoff for efficient portfolios (combining risk-free asset)

**218. Security market line (SML):** CAPM relationship between expected return and beta

**219. Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT):** Diversification optimization (Markowitz)

**220. Post-Modern Portfolio Theory (PMPT):** Focuses on downside risk

**221. Behavioral finance examines:** Psychological biases affecting decisions

**222. Loss aversion:** Pain of loss > pleasure of equivalent gain

**223. Overconfidence bias:** Overestimating knowledge/abilities

**224. Confirmation bias:** Seeking/favoring confirming information

**225. Herd behavior:** Following crowd without independent analysis

**226. Mental accounting:** Treating money differently based on source/purpose

**227. Anchoring:** Relying too heavily on initial information

**228. Recency bias:** Overweighting recent events

**229. Gambler's fallacy:** Believing past independent events affect future probabilities

**230. Risk management process:** Identify, measure, monitor, control, report

**231. Hedging strategies:** Using derivatives to offset risks

**232. Asset-liability management (ALM):** Matching assets to liabilities

**233. Duration matching:** Aligning asset/liability duration

**234. Immunization strategy:** Creating portfolio resistant to interest rate changes

**235. Contingent immunization:** Active management unless triggers require immunization

**236. Risk budgeting:** Allocating risk across investments/strategies

**237. Value at Risk (VaR) methods:** Historical, parametric, Monte Carlo

**238. Backtesting VaR:** Comparing predicted vs actual losses

**239. Greeks for options risk:** Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega, Rho

**240. Basis risk:** Hedge not perfectly correlated with underlying

**241. Gap analysis:** Assessing interest rate risk from timing differences

**242. Liquidity coverage ratio (LCR):** High-quality liquid assets ÷ Net cash outflows (30 days)

**243. Net stable funding ratio (NSFR):** Available stable funding ÷ Required stable funding

**244. Counterparty credit risk (CCR):** Risk other party defaults on derivative/contract

**245. Credit valuation adjustment (CVA):** Adjusting derivative value for counterparty risk

**246. Operational risk:** Losses from failed processes, people, systems, external events

**247. Compliance risk:** Violating laws/regulations

**248. Reputational risk:** Damage to reputation affecting business

**249. Strategic risk:** Poor business decisions, failing to adapt
