## üîç Key Legal and Regulatory Concepts

### Fiduciary Responsibility
Investment adviser representatives have a fiduciary duty to their clients, which means:
- Placing client interests ahead of their own
- Making suitable investment recommendations
- Disclosing all material facts and conflicts of interest
- Charging reasonable compensation for services

### Suitability Requirements
When making recommendations, representatives must consider:
- Client's financial situation and needs
- Investment objectives and risk tolerance
- Tax status and liquidity needs
- Other security holdings and overall portfolio

## üìä Practice Question Examples

### Example 1: Economic Concepts
**Question**: Which of the following best describes the "time value of money" concept?

**Answer Choices**:
A. Money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future
B. Inflation always decreases purchasing power over time
C. Interest rates determine currency exchange values
D. Investment returns should be calculated annually

**Correct Answer**: A
**Explanation**: The time value of money principle states that money available now is worth more than the identical sum in the future due to its potential earning capacity.

### Example 2: Regulatory Requirements
**Question**: Under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940, which of the following must be provided to clients annually?

**Answer Choices**:
A. Balance sheet
B. Form ADV Part 2
C. Performance report
D. Tax documentation

**Correct Answer**: B
**Explanation**: Registered investment advisers must deliver Form ADV Part 2 (brochure) to clients annually, which discloses information about the adviser's business practices, fees, and conflicts of interest.
