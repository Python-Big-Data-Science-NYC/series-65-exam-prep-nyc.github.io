## Part 1: Suitability Standards (Questions 1-40)

### **Question 1**
**Question:** Under FINRA Rule 2111, what is the primary consideration when determining if an investment recommendation is suitable for a client?
**Answer Choices:**
A. The investment's potential for high returns
B. The firm's profitability on the transaction
C. The client's investment profile and financial situation
D. The investment's historical performance over 10 years
**Correct Answer:** C. The client's investment profile and financial situation
**Explanation:** FINRA Rule 2111 requires that recommendations be based on the client's investment profile, which includes financial situation, risk tolerance, investment objectives, and other relevant factors.

---

### **Question 2**
**Question:** A 75-year-old retiree living on fixed income seeks recommendations. Which investment would MOST LIKELY be unsuitable?
**Answer Choices:**
A. AAA-rated municipal bonds
B. Blue-chip dividend-paying stocks
C. Leveraged real estate investment trust (REIT)
D. Short-term Treasury bills
**Correct Answer:** C. Leveraged real estate investment trust (REIT)
**Explanation:** Leveraged REITs carry higher risk and volatility inappropriate for a retiree dependent on fixed income who needs capital preservation and stable income.

---

### **Question 3**
**Question:** Which of the following is NOT a required component of a client's investment profile under suitability rules?
**Answer Choices:**
A. Age and investment time horizon
B. Liquidity needs
C. Tax status
D. Marital status
E. Risk tolerance
**Correct Answer:** D. Marital status
**Explanation:** While marital status might be relevant for some planning, it is not a specifically required component under FINRA Rule 2111, which mandates consideration of age, investment experience, time horizon, liquidity needs, risk tolerance, and tax status.

---

### **Question 4**
**Question:** A client with a "moderate" risk tolerance and 10-year time horizon experiences a 15% portfolio decline. They call you in a panic wanting to sell everything. What is your BEST course of action?

A. Execute the sell order immediately to prevent further losses
B. Refuse the order as it violates their stated risk tolerance
C. Discuss market volatility and their long-term objectives before proceeding

**Correct Answer:** C. Discuss market volatility and their long-term objectives before proceeding
**Explanation:** The investment advisor should educate the client about market cycles, reassess if their risk tolerance was misstated, and ensure any decision aligns with their long-term goals before executing transactions.

## Part 2: Portfolio Management & Asset Allocation (Questions 41-80)

### **Question 41**
**Question:** Modern Portfolio Theory emphasizes:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Picking individual winning stocks
B. Market timing
C. Diversification to reduce unsystematic risk
D. Technical analysis patterns
**Correct Answer:** C. Diversification to reduce unsystematic risk
**Explanation:** Harry Markowitz's Modern Portfolio Theory shows diversification reduces portfolio volatility without necessarily reducing returns by eliminating unsystematic (company-specific) risk.

---

### **Question 42**
**Question:** The efficient frontier represents:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Portfolios with highest returns regardless of risk
B. Portfolios with lowest risk regardless of returns
C. Optimal portfolios offering highest return for given risk level
D. Only index fund portfolios
**Correct Answer:** C. Optimal portfolios offering highest return for given risk level
**Explanation:** The efficient frontier graphically shows portfolios that maximize expected return for a given level of risk or minimize risk for a given return.

---

### **Question 43**
**Question:** Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) formula: Expected Return = Risk-Free Rate + Beta × (Market Return - Risk-Free Rate). If risk-free rate = 2%, market return = 8%, beta = 1.2, expected return is:
**Answer Choices:**
A. 8.0%
B. 9.2%
C. 10.4%
D. 12.0%
**Correct Answer:** B. 9.2%
**Explanation:** 2% + 1.2 × (8% - 2%) = 2% + 1.2 × 6% = 2% + 7.2% = 9.2%

---

### **Question 44**
**Question:** Beta measures:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Total risk of a security
B. Systematic risk relative to market
C. Company-specific risk
D. Dividend growth rate
**Correct Answer:** B. Systematic risk relative to market
**Explanation:** Beta measures a stock's volatility relative to the overall market (systematic risk). Beta = 1 means moves with market, <1 less volatile, >1 more volatile.

---

### **Question 45**
**Question:** Standard deviation measures:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Average return
B. Total risk (both systematic and unsystematic)
C. Only downside risk
D. Correlation between assets
**Correct Answer:** B. Total risk (both systematic and unsystematic)
**Explanation:** Standard deviation measures total volatility/risk of an investment, encompassing both systematic (market) and unsystematic (specific) risk.

---

### **Question 46**
**Question:** Sharpe Ratio formula measures:
**Answer Choices:**
A. Return per unit of total risk
B. Alpha generation
C. Tracking error
D. Dividend yield
**Correct Answer:** A. Return per unit of total risk
**Explanation:** Sharpe Ratio = (Portfolio Return - Risk-Free Rate) ÷ Standard Deviation. Higher ratio = better risk-adjusted return.

---

### **Question 47**

### **Question 61-80: Quick Portfolio Management Questions**

**61. Monte Carlo simulation tests:** Probability of success under random market scenarios

**62. "Glide path" in target-date funds:** Gradually becomes more conservative approaching target date

**63. "Home bias" is:** Overweighting domestic versus international investments

**64. "Currency hedging" removes:** Foreign exchange fluctuation risk

**65. "Duration" measures:** Bond price sensitivity to interest rate changes

**66. Convexity measures:** Curvature of price-yield relationship (refines duration)

**67. "Barbell" bond strategy:** Combines short and long maturities, avoids intermediate

**68. "Bullet" bond strategy:** Concentrates in intermediate maturities

**69. "Ladder" bond strategy:** Spreads maturities evenly over time

**70. "Contango" in futures:** Future prices higher than spot (normal market)

**71. "Backwardation" in futures:** Future prices lower than spot

**73. "Tracking error" measures:** Deviation from benchmark index

**74. "Information ratio" measures:** Alpha per unit of tracking error

**75. "Jensen's alpha" adjusts for:** Risk using CAPM (excess return over expected)

**77. "Maximum drawdown" measures:** Largest peak-to-trough decline

**78. "Value at Risk (VaR)" estimates:** Maximum loss over period at confidence level

**79. "Conditional VaR (CVaR)" measures:** Average loss beyond VaR threshold

**80. "Stress testing" examines:** Portfolio under extreme historical or hypothetical events

**118. "Foreign tax credit" avoids:** Double taxation on international investments

## Summary & Key Formulas

### **Suitability Formulas:**
1. Required Yield = Annual Income Need ÷ Portfolio Value
2. Risk-adjusted Return = (Return - Risk-Free Rate) ÷ Risk Measure
3. Sustainable Withdrawal Rate ≈ 4% of initial portfolio (inflation-adjusted)

### **Portfolio Formulas:**
1. Expected Return (CAPM) = Rf + β(Rm - Rf)
2. Portfolio β = Σ(wi × βi)
3. Sharpe Ratio = (Rp - Rf) ÷ σp
4. Correlation range: -1.0 to +1.0

### **Tax Formulas:**
1. Taxable Equivalent Yield = Tax-Exempt Yield ÷ (1 - Tax Rate)
2. After-Tax Return = Pre-Tax Return × (1 - Tax Rate)
3. Capital Gains Tax = Gain × Applicable Rate (0%/15%/20%)

### **Retirement Formulas:**
1. Rule of 72: Years to Double = 72 ÷ Annual Return %
2. Retirement Needs = Annual Expenses × 25 (4% rule inverse)
3. Social Security Reduction = 5/9% per month early (up to 36 months), then 5/12% per month

### **Estate Planning:**
1. Tentative Tax = (Taxable Estate × Rate) - Unified Credit
2. Annual Exclusion Gifts: $18,000 per recipient (2024)
3. Step-up Basis = Fair Market Value at Death
