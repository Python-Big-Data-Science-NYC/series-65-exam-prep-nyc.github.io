## **Unit 1: Laws, Regulations & Guidelines – 100 Advanced Practice Questions**

### **Question 1**
Under the Securities Act of 1933, which security would be exempt from registration but NOT exempt from antifraud provisions?
A. Municipal bonds issued by a city
B. Commercial paper with 270-day maturity
C. Insurance policies issued by a state-regulated insurer
D. Bank securities approved by the Comptroller of the Currency

**Correct Answer:** B. Commercial paper with 270-day maturity

**Explanation:** Commercial paper with maturity ≤270 days is exempt from registration under the 1933 Act but remains subject to antifraud provisions. All securities transactions, whether exempt from registration or not, are subject to antifraud rules under federal securities laws.

---

### **Question 2**
Under Regulation D, Rule 506(b), which investor does NOT count toward the 35 non-accredited investor limit?
A. A director of the issuer
B. A non-accredited investor who receives adequate information
C. A purchaser who meets only sophistication requirements
D. All non-accredited investors count toward the limit

**Correct Answer:** A. A director of the issuer

**Explanation:** In Rule 506(b) offerings, "purchasers who are directors, executive officers, or general partners of the issuer" are excluded from the count of non-accredited investors for the 35-person limit.

---

### **Question 3**
Under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, which entity is required to register as a broker-dealer?
A. A finder who receives transaction-based compensation
B. A bank that executes occasional securities transactions
C. An issuer selling only its own securities to employees
D. A person effecting transactions in exempt securities only

**Correct Answer:** A. A finder who receives transaction-based compensation

**Explanation:** Anyone engaged in the business of effecting securities transactions for compensation must register as a broker-dealer under the 1934 Act, including finders receiving transaction-based compensation.

### **Question 4**
Under the USA, which security transaction would be exempt from state registration but NOT exempt from antifraud provisions?
A. Unsolicited order by a customer
B. Private placement to 10 state residents
C. Transaction with an institutional investor
D. All of the above

**Correct Answer:** D. All of the above

**Explanation:** Under the Uniform Securities Act, these transactions may be exempt from registration requirements but remain subject to antifraud provisions. Antifraud rules apply to all securities transactions.

### **Question 5**
An investment adviser with $125 million in AUM has its principal office in California and clients in 5 other states. Under the Investment Advisers Act, the firm:
A. Must register with the SEC and notice file in California
B. May choose either SEC or state registration
C. Must register with the SEC only
D. Must register with California and each state with >5 clients

**Correct Answer:** A. Must register with the SEC and notice file in California

**Explanation:** At $125 million AUM, federal registration is mandatory. The adviser must also notice file in its home state (California) and any other states where it has more than 5 clients (subject to de minimis exemptions).

### **Question 6**
Under the 1934 Act, which is NOT considered a manipulative practice?
A. Marking the close
B. Matched orders

**Correct Answer:** C. Stabilizing during a distribution

**Explanation:** Stabilizing during a distribution is permitted under SEC rules when properly disclosed. Marking the close, matched orders, and painting the tape are all prohibited manipulative practices.
